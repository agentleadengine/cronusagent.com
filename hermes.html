Η Hermes

The agent that grows with you. Hermes does not just complete tasks - it learns from them: it writes its own skills from experience, improves them as it works, and builds a deepening picture of how you like things done.

What it is

Hermes is an open-source, self-improving agent built by Nous Research, and it is the only major agent with a true built-in learning loop. When Hermes finishes a job, it can turn what it did into a reusable skill - a written playbook it keeps forever. Next time similar work comes up, it draws on that skill and executes faster and better. It also remembers across sessions: it searches its own past conversations and builds a model of who you are and how you work.

It runs on your machine (or any server you own), stores everything locally, and carries a serious toolkit: web browsing and search, files, scheduled automations, subagents it can spawn for parallel work, and any tool you connect through MCP.

Why that matters

Every AI tool is smart on day one. Almost none are smarter on day thirty.

A normal assistant starts from zero every morning. Hermes compounds: the skills it wrote last week, the lessons it kept, the picture of you it has built - all of it is loaded and working on day thirty-one. Compounding is the difference between a toy and a coworker.

What it is good at

  • Work you repeat. Outreach, follow-ups, reports, weekly routines - anything you do more than once is exactly where a learning agent pulls ahead, because run ten is built on runs one through nine.
  • Learning YOUR way. Skills are not generic: they encode how you write, what you consider done, which steps your business actually uses. Teach it once, or let it learn by doing.
  • Long-running projects. Because it remembers across sessions, Hermes can carry multi-week work without losing the thread.
  • Skills you can inspect and share. Its skills are portable files, compatible with the open agent-skills format, shareable through a community skills hub.

How Cronus uses it

  • Routed. Ask Cronus for something outreach- or routine-shaped and it will usually pick Hermes, tell you so, and run the job in the background.
  • Direct. Click the cyan Η chip in the dock and talk straight to Hermes in its own window, output streaming live.

Fair warning on speed: each fresh conversation, Hermes boots its full toolkit - tools, memory, skills. The first reply can take a minute. That is the cost of an agent that shows up fully loaded, and making it faster is on the roadmap.

Hermes vs OpenClaw, in one line

Hermes writes its own skills by learning from your work. OpenClaw imports ready-made skills from a community market of thousands. They are complementary, which is exactly why Cronus gives you both on one desk.

Do I need it?

No. Cronus works with whatever agents you have; without Hermes, its dock chip just shows an honest gray dot. If you want it, it is free, open source, and the setup wizard's health check points you at what is missing.
