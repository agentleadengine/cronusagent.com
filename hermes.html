Η Hermes

The learner. Hermes is an agent that grows around you: teach it a skill once and it keeps it forever, remembers what worked, and gets better at your work every time it runs.

What it is

Hermes is a separate open agent program that Cronus connects to. What makes it different from every chat tool you have used: it learns. Hermes has a skill system - teachable playbooks that encode how YOU do things - and its own long-term memory. Show it your way of writing a follow-up or running a weekly routine once, and that becomes a permanent ability it applies on every future run.

It also carries its own toolkit (email, browsing, files, calendars, whatever you connect through MCP), so a learned skill is not just knowledge - it is something Hermes can actually go do.

Cronus does not replace Hermes or wrap it in a cage. It just gives Hermes a desk: a window to talk to it directly, a router that hands it jobs that fit, and a health check that tells you honestly whether it is installed and working.

What it is good at

  • Learning your skills. A skill is a written playbook for a job - your voice rules for emails, your steps for a weekly report. Add one and Hermes follows it, every time, without being reminded.
  • Getting better with use. Its memory keeps the lessons from every run, so the tenth campaign genuinely beats the first. (See how memory works.)
  • Outreach, email, and routines - the work it most commonly carries: drafting, follow-ups, multi-step recurring jobs.
  • Tool-heavy tasks - it speaks MCP, the open standard for connecting AI to outside tools, so its reach grows with whatever you plug in.
Why learning matters more than raw smarts

Every AI tool is smart on day one. Almost none are smarter on day thirty. An agent that accumulates your skills and your lessons compounds - and compounding is the difference between a toy and a coworker.

How Cronus uses it

Two ways, same as every agent:

  • Routed. Ask Cronus for something outreach-shaped ("draft a follow-up for the leads that never replied") and it will usually pick Hermes, tell you so, and run the job in the background while you do other things.
  • Direct. Click the cyan Η chip in the dock. What you type goes straight to Hermes, and its output streams into the window live.
Why is the first Hermes reply slow?

Each fresh conversation, Hermes boots its full toolkit: every tool connection, its memory, its skills. That can take a minute. It is the price of an agent that arrives fully equipped rather than one that fakes readiness. Making this faster is on the roadmap.

Do I need it?

No. Cronus works with whatever agents you have. If Hermes is not installed, its dock chip shows a gray dot and everything else works normally. If you want it, the setup wizard's health check will point you at what is missing.
