Watching: honest status, real audits

Watch: trust what you can see · 56 seconds · animated, sound on

A comforting dashboard is a dangerous dashboard

You cannot trust answers from a stack you cannot see. Cronus would rather show you a gray dot than pretend a broken thing is fine.

Status that tells the truth

Tool Health shows every connected piece of your stack. Green means working, tested moments ago, not assumed from a config file. Gray means not connected, which is normal on any machine and never dressed up as anything else. When something breaks, you find out from the dashboard, not from a wrong answer three days later.

The paper trail

Every job leaves a record in the Activity log: what ran, which agent ran it, what came back. If you cannot see what ran, you cannot check it, and checking is the whole game.

The audit that tells on your setup

The Safety page runs a read-only scan over each agent's real configuration files and flags what it finds: risky settings like pre-approved destructive commands or forgotten auto-approve flags, each with a severity, the exact file it lives in, and a plain-words suggestion for what to do.

The first line of that page is the design promise: it only looks. It never changes your settings. The findings are yours to act on, or not.
