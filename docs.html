The idea in one minute

You probably use more than one AI tool. Each lives in its own tab, terminal, or app, and none of them know about each other. Cronus puts them on one desk.

There are two ways to work:

  • Ask Cronus. Type anything into the main chat. Cronus answers small things itself, and for real work it picks the agent whose skills fit and hands the job over - telling you which one it chose and why.
  • Go direct. The dock at the bottom right shows every agent. Click one and it opens as its own window. What you type there goes straight to that agent, no routing, and you watch its output live.
Η Hermes - automation, email, outreach Κ Claude - code and deep work Ω OpenClaw - browser missions

Everything runs on your computer. The dashboard is a local page only you can reach, and your files, credentials, and chat history never leave your machine unless you connect a cloud model on purpose.

Your first five minutes

  1. Install and start it (see Install). The dashboard opens at localhost:8787/cronus, or in its own window if you use the desktop app.
  2. Meet the setup wizard. On a fresh machine Cronus opens Setup first. It health-checks your computer: green dot means working, gray dot means not connected yet. Gray is normal on a new machine - every gray row includes a fix hint in plain words.
  3. Connect one brain. Chat needs at least one AI model. The free path: install Ollama and run ollama pull qwen3:4b. Cronus spots it automatically and lists it as "local, free".
  4. Say something. Go to the chat and ask "what can you do?". The first reply on a local model is slow while the model loads into memory. After that it gets quicker.

How to: talk to Cronus

The main chat (called Oracle in the sidebar) is the front door. Cronus reads your message and does one of four things:

  • Answers directly - small talk, questions about your setup, things it already knows.
  • Delegates - picks ONE agent, hands the job over as a background task, and reports back. You can watch the run in the Log.
  • Asks one question - only when your request genuinely has two readings and guessing would waste your time.
  • Handles your to-dos - if you connected TickTick, "what's due today" or "add a task" works right in chat.

The model chip in the top bar shows which brain Cronus is using for its own thinking. Click it to switch.

How to: talk to one agent directly

Click an agent's chip in the dock (bottom right). Its window opens with its own conversation. This is a direct line: Cronus does not route, rewrite, or supervise anything you type there.

  • Windows drag by the title bar, stack, and remember their conversation even after you close them.
  • While the agent works, its raw output streams into the window live - useful for watching long jobs.
  • A green dot on the chip means the agent's software is installed on this machine. Gray means it is not - the window will tell you what is missing rather than pretending.
Why both a router and direct lines?

Sometimes you do not care who does the job - that is what asking Cronus is for. Sometimes you know exactly which agent you want and the middleman is friction. Both are first-class, and neither locks you out of the other.

How to: pick your model

The Model menu in the top bar lists every brain Cronus can find, in three flavors:

FlavorWhat it meansCost
local, freeA model running on your own computer through Ollama. Private, works offline.Free forever
Claude planAnthropic's models through your Claude subscription, once you sign in.Your existing plan
cloudModels billed through other accounts you have connected.Depends on the account

Rule of thumb: local models are private and free but slower and simpler. Claude models are fast and sharp but need a subscription. Cronus falls back gracefully - if your chosen model is unreachable, it tries the next sane option and tells you what happened.

Want another local model? The Local Models page lets you download one by name (like llama3.2:3b) and shows what fits in your computer's memory.

The other pages, in one line each

  • Loom - have Cronus summarize a past conversation and hand the context to another agent.
  • Tasks - your TickTick to-dos, due and overdue, editable in place.
  • Automations - describe a routine in plain words, get a draft workflow.
  • Browser Agent - give it a site and a goal, it clicks through the page for you.
  • Leads - paste messy lead notes, get a ranked call-first list with talking points.
  • Scheduled Runs - jobs on a timer, like a morning inbox summary.
  • Documents and Knowledge - drop files or folders in, then ask questions about what is inside.
  • Doc Extract - turn a scanned invoice or messy PDF into clean structured facts.
  • Add a Tool, Plugins, MCP Servers - connect outside tools and see what is on and reachable.
  • Credentials - saved keys, encrypted; once saved, the value is never shown again.
  • Activity, Daily Brief, Tool Health - what is running, what needs you, what is down.

How to: change how it looks

The style button in the top bar changes the entire app: font pack, accent color, corner sharpness, spacing density, glow. Pick, save, done. It is your desk - set it how you like it.

Why local-first

Cronus runs on your machine and answers only to you. There is no Cronus cloud, no account to create, no telemetry phoning home, and the app never updates itself behind your back - new versions are installers you choose to install.

The trade we chose

Cloud apps are easier to ship and easier to bill. But an orchestrator sees everything - your tasks, your leads, your documents, your keys. We think that thing should live where you can unplug it.

Why a free model by default

You should not need a credit card to find out whether Cronus is useful. A small local model is genuinely enough for routing, summaries, and everyday questions. When you want more horsepower, connecting your Claude plan is one sign-in, and Cronus will use it without ceremony.

Why so many gray dots

On a fresh machine, most integrations show gray "not connected yet" states. That is on purpose. Cronus never fakes a connection, never hides a failure behind a spinner, and every error tries to tell you the next step in plain words. Software that pretends everything is fine is software you cannot trust with real work.

Common questions

Chat is slow.

You are probably on a small local model - the first reply after a restart also loads the model into memory, which is the slowest moment. Options: keep chatting (it warms up), pull a smaller model, or sign in to a Claude plan for fast replies.

An agent's chip has a gray dot.

That agent's software is not installed on this machine. Cronus works fine with whatever subset you have - the dock just tells you the truth about what is present.

My antivirus flagged the installer.

The prebuilt installers are not yet code-signed, and antivirus tools distrust unsigned new programs on principle. The code is open source and reviewable. Installing from source avoids the warnings entirely; signing is on the roadmap.

Where is my data?

In your home folder: app state lives in ~/artemis, and background job logs in ~/.cache/prompt-studio-jobs. Delete those and the repo folder, and Cronus is fully gone.

Something broke. Where do I look?

Open Setup and run the health check - it says what is working and how to fix what is not, row by row. Still stuck? Open an issue on GitHub and paste what the health check said.
