The composer

The box you type into learned three tricks: skip the router with an @-mention, get your prompt graded before you send it, and let the composer fix a weak prompt for you.

narrated · sound on

Why this matters

Check the ask before it runs

Most AI failures are prompt failures. A strict grade and a one-click fix, applied before anything executes, is the cheapest quality gate that exists - and it teaches you to write better asks as a side effect.

You will spend hours a week at this desk. A look you chose, a palette that jumps anywhere in a keystroke, and an @-mention that skips the router - each saves seconds that compound into wanting to be here.

The walkthrough

@-mentions: straight to an agent

Start a message with @hermes, @claude, or @openclaw and it bypasses the router - the fastest direct line in the app, no window needed.

The A± button: grade before you send

Click A± and a deliberately cheap local judge grades your prompt A through F - strictly, on purpose. The card breaks the score down (task, context, output, structure, examples, constraints) and tells you exactly what is missing, with concrete suggestions instead of vibes. A vague prompt earns its F.

Fix it for me

One button on the grade card rewrites your prompt with the missing details filled in. You review the improved version before it sends - the composer proposes, you decide.

Drafts that survive

Half-typed messages persist - close the app mid-thought and your draft is waiting when you come back.
