Foresight

Hindsight remembers what you do. Foresight reads those memories looking for the work you keep doing by hand - and proposes automations for it.

narrated · sound on

Why this matters

Memory that pays rent

Remembering is only step one. A stack that notices patterns in its own memory and offers to automate them completes the loop: remember, notice, automate. That is compounding you can watch happen.

The walkthrough

Scan your memory

One button: scan. Foresight reads your accumulated memories - not your files, not your keystrokes, the memories your agents already keep - and looks for repeated manual work.

Read the proposals

Each finding comes as a proposal: what pattern it noticed, what an automation would do, and a rough time saving. The estimates are labeled as estimates with a stated basis - never dressed up as measurements. Honesty applies to promises too.

Accept, ignore, or edit

Proposals are suggestions, not actions. Accept one and it becomes a real automation - a scheduled run or workflow born from your own history instead of a template store. Ignore the ones that miss.

Come back monthly

Foresight gets better as memory accumulates. A quiet scan at the end of each month tends to surface one genuine automation you did not think to ask for.
