Memory

Models forget you the moment a chat ends. Memory is the fix - and in Cronus it lives on your machine, in files you own.

narrated · sound on

Why this matters

Compounding is the product

Every AI tool is smart on day one. Almost none are smarter on day thirty. An assistant that keeps lessons compounds - and compounding is the difference between a tool and a coworker.

The walkthrough

The Memory page

Your window into the memory service. If it is not running, the page says exactly that - honest gray, never fake green - with a check-again button. When it runs, agents retain what mattered from their work and recall it when it becomes relevant.

What actually gets remembered

Not transcripts - distillations. Preferences ("no hype words"), facts ("the lease renews in March"), and lessons ("the question-first follow-up got replies"). You can teach memory explicitly by saying it once, or let agents learn by doing.

Foresight: memory that notices

The Foresight page reads your accumulated memories looking for repeated manual work, and proposes automations for it - with time estimates that are labeled as estimates, never dressed up as measurements. Your stack does not just remember; it notices patterns you did not.

Yours, on disk

Memories are files on your machine. Read them, back them up, delete them - and if you ever switch model providers, your memory comes with you. No platform holds your brain hostage.
