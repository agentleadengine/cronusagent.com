Watching your stack

The last habit Cronus teaches is watching. Five pages exist so that nothing in your stack ever happens where you cannot see it.

narrated · sound on

Why this matters

Trust is a feature you can inspect

Software that pretends everything is fine is software you cannot trust with real work. Every page in this guide is a different way of seeing the truth - which is exactly what makes delegation to agents survivable.

The walkthrough

Activity: who is working right now

Every background job appears as a little worker at a desk - a job walks in when it starts and walks out when it is done. Glance here whenever you wonder what your agents are doing this second.

Tool Health: the truth about your setup

Pings every part of your stack and reports in plain english: working, not set up yet, or broken - with a fix hint for each. Green means tested seconds ago, never assumed.

Safety: a read-only audit

The Safety page scans your agents' own configurations and ranks findings from high to low. It only looks - nothing is ever changed automatically. You decide what to fix and when.

Guardrails: autonomy with a dial

Per agent, guardrails set how much it may do on its own before it must stop and ask you - always ask, ask when unfamiliar, or proceed. Capability never has to mean autonomy.

Scheduled Runs: the morning brief

Put the watching itself on a timer. The built-in morning brief reads what your agents did overnight and writes the short version: what got done, what failed, and what needs you. Coffee, brief, done.
